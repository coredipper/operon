\section{Implementation and Experiments}\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Reference Implementation}

The correspondence is implemented in the Operon framework (\texttt{operon-ai}, MIT license). Key components:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Architecture extraction}: \texttt{extract\_architecture(organism)} produces the $(G, \mathrm{Know}, \Phi)$ triple from any \texttt{SkillOrganism}.
\item \textbf{Compiler functors}: Five \texttt{CompilerFunctor} instances with automatic preservation verification.
\item \textbf{Certificate framework}: Self-verifying certificates with \texttt{certify()} and \texttt{verify()} methods on certifiable components (ATP budget, quorum sensing, mTOR scaling).
\item \textbf{Atomic skills catalog}: \texttt{seed\_library\_from\_atomic\_skills()} registers the five skills with correct topology mapping.
\item \textbf{LangGraph compiler}: \texttt{organism\_to\_langgraph()} creates one LangGraph node per stage, each calling \texttt{run\_single\_stage()}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Escalation Experiment}

To test whether structural guarantees are genuinely harness-level, we designed an experiment that changes the model while keeping the harness constant.

\paragraph{Setup.} A single-stage code review organism with:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{Fast model}: Phi-3 Mini (3.8B parameters) via Ollama
\item \textbf{Deep model}: Gemma 4 (27B MoE, 4B active) via Ollama
\item \textbf{VerifierComponent}: Rubric-based quality evaluation using Gemma~4 as judge
\item \textbf{WatcherComponent}: Convergence detection with quality-based escalation
\item \textbf{Quality threshold}: 0.6 (below this, the watcher fires ESCALATE)
\end{itemize}

The task is \texttt{hard\_par\_08}: a code review containing four subtle bugs (off-by-one pagination, TOCTOU cache race, float precision in financial math, \texttt{except Exception} swallowing \texttt{KeyboardInterrupt}).

\paragraph{Results.}

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lccl@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Phase} & \textbf{Model} & \textbf{Quality} & \textbf{Action} \\
\midrule
Initial execution & Phi-3 Mini (fast) & 0.50 & EXECUTE \\
Verifier evaluation & Gemma 4 (judge) & --- & Score = 0.50 \\
Watcher decision & --- & $0.50 < 0.60$ & ESCALATE \\
Escalated execution & Gemma 4 (deep) & --- & EXECUTE \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

The escalation fires correctly: Phi-3 Mini produces a review that the judge scores below threshold, the watcher escalates to Gemma~4, and the deep model re-executes the stage. The structural guarantee (quality-based escalation) operates independently of which model executes---it is a property of the harness ($\mathrm{Know}$), not the model ($\Phi$).

\paragraph{Discrimination.} Independent validation of the task confirms discrimination between weak and strong models: Phi-3 Mini scores 0.72 (identifies bug classes but not concrete failure scenarios), Gemma~4 scores 1.00 (explains each failure scenario in detail). Quality delta = 0.28, exceeding the 0.20 threshold.

\subsection{SWE-bench-lite: Decomposition Does Not Help on Real Bug-Fix Tasks}
\label{sec:swebench-phase2}

To test whether the categorical harness delivers end-to-end value beyond certificate preservation, we ran an apples-to-apples comparison on SWE-bench-lite: 10 real Python bug-fix instances from \texttt{astropy} and \texttt{django}, with patches applied inside the official SWE-bench Docker harness and evaluated via \texttt{FAIL\_TO\_PASS} + \texttt{PASS\_TO\_PASS} test suites.

\paragraph{Setup.} Gemma~4 (27B MoE, 4B active) via Ollama. Three conditions:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{baseline}: direct single prompt, request unified diff.
\item \textbf{organism}: three-stage \texttt{SkillOrganism} (\textit{localize} $\to$ \textit{edit} $\to$ \textit{verify}).
\item \textbf{langgraph}: same organism, compiled via \texttt{organism\_to\_langgraph()} and executed as a LangGraph \texttt{StateGraph}.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Results (pass/fail, 10 instances).}

\begin{center}
\small
\begin{tabular}{@{}lccc@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Condition} & \textbf{Resolved} & \textbf{Patch extracted} & \textbf{Mean latency} \\
\midrule
baseline    & 0/10 & \textbf{10/10} & 44~s  \\
organism    & 0/10 & 6/10           & 88~s  \\
langgraph   & 0/10 & 6/10           & 90~s  \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\paragraph{Interpretation.} All three conditions resolve zero instances. This is an honest negative result about the \emph{model}, not the harness: Gemma~4 27B (4B active) is too weak to pass SWE-bench-lite unaided on either prompt style. Two secondary findings are load-bearing, however:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
\item \textbf{The organism and LangGraph pipelines \emph{degrade} patch extraction} (6/10 vs baseline's 10/10). Direct prompting produces cleaner unified diffs than a three-stage localize--edit--verify pipeline. Pipeline overhead introduces format drift.
\item \textbf{Latency doubles with no offsetting gain.} The three-stage pipeline costs $\sim$2$\times$ baseline wall-clock for identical pass/fail outcomes.
\end{enumerate}

A judge-based Phase~1 evaluation of the same instances (LLM-as-judge over a plausibility rubric) reported baseline=0.808 and organism=0.776, which we interpreted as plausibility of the produced diff. The pass/fail truth at the harness level collapses that judge signal: when a patch cannot be applied at all (\texttt{error}) or is applied but fails tests (\texttt{unresolved}), the judge's plausibility score is not predictive of correctness.

\paragraph{What this result does and does not say.} It says: categorical compilation and multi-stage decomposition do \emph{not} substitute for model capability on hard, mechanistic tasks. This is consistent with the empirical finding in the companion Bio-Benchmarks paper that decomposition-based abstractions do not earn their complexity for capable models on hard SWE-bench instances; we extend that to: they do not help small models either. The harness-level \emph{structural} guarantees (certificate preservation, priority gating, stagnation detection) remain valid under compilation regardless of model capability --- but guarantees about \emph{structure} do not imply gains in \emph{task resolution}.

This motivates Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}: the transfer value of categorical harness engineering is in \emph{reliability primitives} (gates, certificates, monitors) that a user can drop into an existing graph, not in wholesale organism adoption.
